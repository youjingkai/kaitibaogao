开题报告



一、研究问题


本研究关注陕西本土作家文学作品中及陕西本地人的性别观念。


二、研究目的


2.1 兴趣源起


年少时一次春节聚餐，和亲人探讨到了关于代孕的问题。令人震惊的是，我的一位叔叔居然说到“女人生来就应该生孩子，不生孩子的女人或者有什么用？”我当时震惊了，我不理解在21世纪居然还有人怀有这种死板且错误的观念。在我的记忆中，我的家庭一直是一个十分开明的家庭，父母会早早地让我领略不同的文化，经常告诉我要多角度思考问题，日常家庭生活中的文学氛围也十分浓厚。而正是在一个如此让人骄傲的家庭中，我的身边 居然还存在拥有如此迂腐的性别观念之人。通过之后的观察，更加明显的是，我身边的男性家属都对于性别有着最错误死板的“男尊女卑”观念。由此，我通过问卷简单做了一下调查，根据我所获得的土生土长于陕西地区的部分男性数据，我发现其都普遍存有差异较落后的性别观念 。但与此同时，这些个体都存在着很大的差异性。于是，我诞生了一个问题：陕西地区之外的人们性别意识会不同吗？经过联系我及父母亲在上海、广州、成都等地的朋友，经过交谈，我发现不同地区的人们对于性别的认识也有着较大的差异性，那么，这是否和区域的经济状况有关呢？我联想到，可能由于上海作为金融中心，面向国际的时间足够长，于是思想或多或少会受到国际的影响，而陕西接受外国思想的程度没有上海深，导致其地区人民思想相对封闭，于是性别观念相对落后。基于以上铺垫，我认为性别意识的差异可能和地域、经济状况有关 ，而出于人的本能，我又渴望更加了解身边的环境和人，同时希望可以验证猜想的正确性，便萌生了研究陕西性别观念的想法。


2.2 研究可行性 接着，由于疫情影响和资源问题，进行涵盖大面积调查数据的研究则显得不太可能。反而如果通过对于文学作品的研读，寻找一些性别存在的观点则显得更加可行。（ 既然人人都会进行文学作品的阅读，而作家的性别观会长久地影响未来的读者，那么为何不研究一下陕西本土知名作家文学作品中的性别观念呢？）


那么，为何我想要研究文学作品呢？


我以为，作家的作品是完全可以反映出作家本身的性别观念的，而优秀的作家是文化变迁的可靠记录者——也就是说，作家本人是文化塑造的产物、时代的亲历者，也是超越现实的存在。也许会有人说，很多人说作家性别观跟作品有差距，这确实存在。但是，对于这个问题，有以下的解释。首先，有些作家会选择直接表达自己的性别观念。 比如鲁迅的《伤逝》和胡适 《美国的妇人》，他们的作品体现了100年前的进步人士的性别观，他们有关性别的言论和看法直接影响了同时代人的性别观念，还间接影响了社会主义妇女解放的纲领的形成和妇女解放运动家的行动理念。其次，由于陕西本土一些居民的性别观念存在偏差，而一些作家为了满足其读者的偏好以获得更高的销量，则会选择在作品中加入传统的性别观——而这种对于受众群体中含有错误性别观念的妥协，便会大大加强男性在社会中的话语权，从而强化社会不平等现状。虽然如贾平凹这样的严肃文学作家可能并不会为了销量投读者所好，但也正因为严肃文学作家本身具有较强的话语权，他们自身较落后的性别意识会给社会造成更大的影响，甚至往往被认为是某种真理。所以，审视这些作家的性别意识是尤为重要的。第三点，文学作品是基于现实生活中的真实情景而建构的，从而会映射出真实世界的案例。因此，我们可以在文学作品中找到现实情况的影子，甚至是对现实更为深刻的再现。第四点，文学作品能够塑造、影响读者的性别意识。 读者并不能做到完全分辨正确或是错误的性别观念。一些作家在无意中创造出的带有性别歧视的角色，在读者眼中很有可能成为正确的向导，从而引导读者以错误的方式来思考。 那么，基于文学的影响理论，为了深入研究文学对性别意识塑造的真正影响力，我邀请身边同龄人参加我的“陕西地区男性作家文学作品研讨会”，旨在通过交流深入理解部分陕西文学作品，挖掘陕西作家的性别意识，同时收集大众对于此类文学作品的看法（尤其是从性别的角度）。


中国文学，包括诗歌和小说，传统上都是承载作者志向、思想的载体。陕西文学作为中国文学的一个分支，则作为本土文学发展成为当地文化的重要载体。 它根植于陕西地区深厚的传统文化，深受中国现实主义文学传统的影响。除此之外，陕西地处内陆，农业文明根深蒂固，比较晚受到商业文明和外来文明的影响。所以陕西文学持久地关注着中国农民的历史命运，不竭地进行着乡土灵魂的探索，而这也让陕西文学在中国“乡土文学”中起到了支柱作用。那么，在基于这种乡土化的文学背景下，受农业文明和传统思想塑造的性别意识便会处处渗透在陕西的文学作品中。比如说，大多数陕西作家对于女性的描写都是侧重于农村女性的，那么这些“农村女性”的形象便很容易就能够体现出作家传统方面的女性观念。


至于为何只研究陕西作家，首先，正如上文所提到的，我以为地域、经济状况等可能会影响性别意识的培养，而陕西作为西北地区的一个代表，作为乡土文学的一个发源地，是十分适合进行这方面的研究的。再者，由于我本身生活在陕西地区，所以进行陕西地区的研究于我而言会更加便捷一些。


三、研究方法


3.1 文本细读：


在文学作品样本的选择中，我首先挑选了一部分陕西地区的经典作家——由于其名声颇高，文学作品则更加有代表性，影响力更加深远。除此之外，我还选择了一些新锐作家——这些作家往往代表着陕西文学新的声音，可以体现出陕西文学随着时代的发展和改变。另外，我分别选择了一些中短篇、长篇的章节，并且在一个作家中尽量选择多篇或一篇长篇作品的多个章节，至此可以更加全面地了解该作家的文学特征，以帮助后续对于作家作品中性别意识的分析。


在具体分析时，我会着重于抓住作品中的暗喻和意象，以叙事结构为线索串联主角或内容的前后矛盾，同时以不同的人称视角入手，以更加全面的分析主题表达。


为了使研究更具有学术意义，性别理论的运用是必要的。在研究中，我会以福柯的社会建构理论为基础，将波伏娃以及巴特勒的性别观念为依据，将”交叉性“等学术概念引进，旨在以更加客观的方式来分析本部作品。


除此之外，为了能够相对较理性地分析作家的性别观念，除本书内容外，我还会收集文外材料进行佐证，通过后记、作家生平、作家经历等方式更加深入了解作家本人。

作品清单：


1、贾平凹《极花》：


“贾平凹是是当代中国一位最具叛逆性、创造精神和广泛影响的作家，他的写作既传统又现代，既写实又高远，语言朴拙、憨厚，内心却波澜万丈”（*茅盾文学奖授奖辞评），*是知名度极高的陕西作家。《极花》从女孩被拐卖到偏远山区的男性家庭开始，用全息体验的方式叙述女孩的遭遇，展示了她所看到的外部世界和经历的内心煎熬。


2、路遥《平凡的世界》：


路遥作为鼓舞亿万农村青年投身改革开放的优秀作家，是陕西作家的代表人物。而《平凡的世界》是中国作家路遥创作的一部全景式地表现中国当代城乡社会生活的百万字长篇小说，以中国70年代中期到80年代中期十年间为背景，通过复杂的矛盾纠葛刻画了当时社会各阶层众多普通人的形象；劳动与爱情、挫折与追求、痛苦与欢乐、日常生活与巨大社会冲突纷繁地交织在一起，深刻地展示了普通人在大时代历史进程中所走过的艰难曲折的道路。


3、陈忠实《白鹿原》：


陈忠实，作为茅盾文学奖获得者，是十分传统且知名的陕西本土作家。《白鹿原》是其代表作，以陕西关中地区白鹿原上白鹿村为缩影，通过讲述白姓和鹿姓两大家族祖孙三代的恩怨纷争，表现了从清朝末年到二十世纪七八十年代长达半个多世纪的历史变化。而当它出版以后，因其尖锐的历史政治观点及大胆的性爱描述，在文学评论界引起了巨大反响和争论。


4、孙亚军《那些远去的身影》：


孙亚军现为陕西携手青年作家协会常务副会长，陕西文学创作研究会理事、陕西民间文艺家协会会员，是具有先锋性的青年作家。他坚信文以载道的古训，崇尚五四一代中国传统学人的风尚，因此作品中含有浓厚的地域特点。


5、陈彦《装台》：


陈彦，现任中国作家协会第十届中国作协副主席、书记处书，是文化部优秀专家，全国宣传文化系统“四个一批人才”。《装台》是由其在2015年创作的小说，荣获中国多个奖项，甚至入选“新中国70年70部长篇小说典藏”。这是陈彦一部十分出名的作品，蕴含着作家代表性的个人风格和意识形态。


在此，我想申明一下本研究的目的。本研究不是为了给陕西的作家的性别意识打分排名，引起大众对作家本人的人身攻击。本研究更大的意义在于发现哪些性别认识在被作家以不同的形式重复，哪些传统的落后的性别意识一直在被宣扬，从而导致其成为陕西地区的普遍认同，潜移默化培养了陕西地区人们的性别意识，将存在的问题合理化。从而提示人们，以更加批判性的角度来思考文学作品中的男女形象。

会议反思：


总结来说，此次书籍研讨会圆满开展。无论是观众的热烈回应，或是整个研讨会核心“两性”的表达，亦或是对于部分作家性别意识的分析及参会者结合自身处境的思考，都有淋漓尽致地体现在此研讨会的进行过程中。然而，此次书籍研讨会也存在着以下问题：


1、会议开始时由于设备和平台的局限性，部分观众产生杂音影响了会议的学术氛围。


2、讨论和个人展示的时间分配不是非常合理，导致会议最终超时。


3、书籍选择的随机性过大，部分书籍选择次数差异较大，导致许多交流内容重复。


针对这些问题，我将会在之后组织的读书交流会中相应改进，争取以举办更加全面精细的研讨活动。


四、研究的局限性


1）由于疫情影响、资源和时间的限制，本研究所涉及到的文学作品和受访者样本并不是足够大、因此，进行涵盖大面积调查数据的研究则显得不太可能，只能选择一部分具有代表性的文学作品、邀请少数几位受访者进行研究。但实际上，女权主义学者Donna Haraway认为，全覆盖、全知全能的研究都犯了所谓“god trick”的错误。不存在绝对客观的视角和绝对客观的研究方法。因为首先研究人员也是一个有主观意识的人，所以所有研究都受到研究者主观意识的影响；其次，研究所涉及的方法、工具，都是特定历史社会条件下的产物。所有的研究都是片面研究。片面研究不代表就是缺乏可信度和意义的。在本研究中，文本精读及研讨会起到相互补充的作用——既有研究者利用性别理论进行的理论化分析，也有受访者作为普通读者，结合自身生活经验得出的阅读反应，这更能加强本研究的可信度。但是为了获得更加精确的数据，当疫情情况好转后，我将会利用假期时间进行更深度、更广泛的调查和研究。


2）本研究选用的是样本抽查法，因此可能无法很好地涉及各个阶级、民族、教育背景、不同经济能力的陕西男性。因此在后续过程中，我将会以不同阶级、民族、教育背景的男性为不同组合，将分层法也加入本研究内容中。





Opening Report




I.Research Questions


This study is concerned with the concept of gender in the literary works of local writers in Shaanxi and among the natives of Shaanxi.


II.Purpose of the Study


2.1 Origin of interest


At a Chinese New Year dinner when I was young, I discussed the issue of surrogacy with my relatives. It was shocking that one of my uncles said, "Women are born to have children; what is the use of a woman who does not have children?" I was shocked and couldn't understand that in the 21st century, there were still people with such rigid and wrong ideas. As far back as I can remember, my family has always been supremely enlightened, with my parents exposing me to different cultures early on, often telling me to think about things from multiple perspectives, and a robust literary atmosphere in my daily family life. Yet, in such a proud family, I was surrounded by people who had pedantic ideas about gender. From my observation, it became even more apparent that the male family members around me had the most misguided and rigid view of "male superiority over female." Thus, I did a brief survey through a questionnaire based on the data I obtained from some of the men born and raised in Shaanxi; I found that they all have different and backward gender concepts. But at the same time, these individuals are very other. So, the question was born: Do people outside Shaanxi have different gender consciousness? After contacting my friends and my parents in Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Chengdu, I found that people's perceptions of gender differed significantly from region to region. I associate it with the fact that maybe because Shanghai, as a financial center, has been facing the international for long enough, the ideas are more or less influenced by the global.

In contrast, Shaanxi has not accepted foreign ideas as profoundly as Shanghai, resulting in the relatively closed mindset of its regional people, so the gender concept is relatively backward. Based on the above, I thought that the difference in gender consciousness might be related to the region and economic status. Out of my human instinct, I wanted to know more about the environment and the people around me. I also wanted to verify the correctness of my conjecture, so I came up with the idea of studying the gender concept in Shaanxi.

 


2.2 Study feasibility 


Next, a study covering a large area of survey data seems unlikely due to the impact of the epidemic and resource issues. So instead, it would be more feasible to look for gender perspectives through literature study. (Since everyone reads literature and the gender perspectives of writers will influence future readers for a long time, why not study the gender perspectives in the literary works of well-known local writers in Shaanxi?)

So, why did I want to study literary works?

I thought that writers' works are perfectly capable of reflecting the writers' gender concepts and those good writers are reliable recorders of cultural change; the writers themselves are products of cultural shaping, pro-lifers of their times, and beings who transcend reality. It may be argued that many people say that there is a gap between the writer's gender perspective and their work, and this does exist. However, there are the following explanations for this problem. First, some writers choose to express their gender concepts directly. For example, Lu Xun's The Hurt Locker and Hu Shih's The American Woman, their works embodied the gender views of progressive people 100 years ago. Their remarks and views about gender directly influenced the gender views of their contemporaries. They also indirectly influenced the formation of the socialist women's liberation program and the action philosophy of women's liberation activists.

Secondly, because of the biased gender concepts of some residents in Shaanxi, some writers choose to include traditional gender views in their works to satisfy the preferences of their readers for higher sales - and this compromise of the audience group contains wrong gender concepts will then significantly strengthen the male discourse in society, thus reinforces the status quo of social inequality. Although serious literary writers like Jia Pingwa may not be in favor of their readers for the sake of sales, it is also because serious academic writers themselves have a strong voice that their backward gender consciousness can have a more significant impact on society and is often even considered to be some kind of truth. Therefore, it is imperative to examine the gender consciousness of these writers. The third point is that literary works are constructed based on real-life scenarios and thus will map out real-world cases. Therefore, we can find shadows of real situations in literary works or even more profound reproductions of reality. The fourth point is that literature can shape and influence readers' gender consciousness. Readers are not able to distinguish between right and wrong gender concepts. Some writers inadvertently create sexist characters that may become correct guides in readers' eyes, leading them to think incorrectly. So, based on the theory of literary influence, I invited my peers to participate in my "Seminar on Literary Works of Male Writers in Shaanxi" to study in depth the real impact of literature on gender consciousness. The workshop aims to share and deepen the understanding of some Shaanxi literary works, explore the gender consciousness of Shaanxi writers, and collect the public's views on such literary works (especially from a gender perspective).

Chinese literature, including poetry and fiction, has traditionally been a vehicle for the author's aspirations and thoughts. Shaanxi literature, as a branch of Chinese literature, has developed as an essential carrier of local culture as local literature. It is rooted in the deep traditional culture of the Shaanxi region and is deeply influenced by the Chinese realist literary tradition. In addition, Shaanxi is located inland, with a deep-rooted agricultural civilization, and was relatively late to be influenced by commercial and foreign cultures. Therefore, Shaanxi literature has been persistently concerned with the historical fate of Chinese peasants and incessantly explored the countryside's soul, making Shaanxi literature a pillar of Chinese "vernacular literature." Based on this vernacular literary background, gender consciousness, shaped by agricultural civilization and traditional thought, permeates Shaanxi literature everywhere. For example, most Shaanxi writers' depictions of women focus on rural women, so these images of "rural women" can quickly reflect the writers' traditional conceptions of women.

As to why I studied only Shaanxi writers, first of all, as mentioned above, I thought that geography and economic conditions might affect the cultivation of gender consciousness, and Shaanxi, as a representative of the northwest region and the birthplace of vernacular literature, is very suitable for this research. Moreover, since I live in Shaanxi, it is more convenient for me to conduct research in Shaanxi.

 

III. Research Methodology

 

3.1 Close reading of texts.

In the selection of the literary sample, I first selected some of the classic writers in Shaanxi - due to their high reputation, their literary works are more representative and far-reaching. In addition, I also decided on some new and emerging writers. These writers often represent new voices in Shaanxi literature and can reflect the development and change of Shaanxi literature with the times. In addition, I have chosen some short and long chapters, respectively, and try to select multiple branches in one writer or multiple components in one long work so that I can get a more comprehensive understanding of the writer's literary characteristics to help the subsequent analysis of gender consciousness in the writer's work.

In the specific analysis, I will focus on capturing the metaphors and imagery in the works, using the narrative structure as a clue to link the main characters or the content's inconsistencies, and starting with different persona perspectives to analyze the thematic expressions more comprehensively.

To make the research more academic, gender theory is necessary. In this study, I will use Foucault's social construction theory as the basis, Beauvoir's and Butler's concepts of gender, and introduce academic concepts such as "intersectionality" to analyze this work more objectively.

In addition, to analyze the writer's concept of gender relatively rationally, I will also collect extra-textual materials to support this book's content and gain a deeper understanding of the writer through the afterword's biography and experience.

IV.Limitations of the study


(1) Due to the impact of the epidemic, resource and time constraints, the sample of literature and respondents involved in this study was not large enough, so it was not possible to conduct a study that covered a large area of survey data, and only a few representative works of literature and a few respondents were invited to conduct the study. But in fact, feminist scholar Donna Haraway argues that all-coverage, all-knowing studies make the mistake of the so-called "god trick. There is no such thing as an objective perspective or an objective methodology. All research is influenced by the subjective consciousness of the researcher because, first of all, the researcher is also an emotional person, so the personal consciousness of the researcher affects all research. All research is one-sided research. However, a one-sided investigation does not mean it lacks credibility and meaning. In this study, the close reading of the text and the workshop play a complementary role - both the theoretical analysis conducted by the researcher using gender theory and the reading responses of the interviewees as ordinary readers with their own life experiences, which can strengthen the credibility of this study. However, to obtain more accurate data, I will use my vacation time to conduct a more in-depth and extensive survey and study when the epidemic situation improves.

 

(2) The sample sampling method chosen for this study may not be able to cover Shaanxi men of all classes, ethnicities, educational backgrounds, and different economic abilities very well. Therefore, in the follow-up process, I will add the stratification method to this study with varying combinations of men of other classes, ethnicities, and educational backgrounds.
